Introduction

The NAVIC satellite system plays a critical role in enhancing weather prediction, navigation, and disaster management across India by providing precise positioning, timing, and regional coverage. Here's a detailed look at how these satellites contribute to each of these field. NAVIC (Navigation with Indian Constellation) is India's regional satellite navigation system developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It is designed to provide accurate positioning and timing information over India and surrounding regions

  • Regional Coverage

    : Unlike GPS, which provides global coverage, NAVIC covers the Indian subcontinent and the surrounding region. This includes India, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and parts of the surrounding Indian Ocean.

  • NAVIC is made up of a constellation of satellites in geostationary and geosynchronous orbits. It consists of: 7 satellites: Three in geostationary orbit and four in geosynchronous orbit. These satellites are strategically placed to ensure coverage over the region .

  • AccuracyNAVIC offers position accuracy of: < 20 meters in the primary service area (India and surrounding regions) < 30 meters for the broader service area.

  • Millitary ApplicationsPrecision navigation and timing for defense applications. Strategic advantages for India in case of national security concerns.
